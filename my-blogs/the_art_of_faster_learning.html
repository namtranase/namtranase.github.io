Why we need fast-learning?

In a world that everything grows and done count by day, we need to push up ourselves to learn more, and especially quickly. But like the computer, the memory of humans is enormous but not unlimited. Our brain is awesome but it needs to sort information in an efficient way. So how we can learn many things, remember almost those things and apply them when we need to?. I will try to figure out the best way that suits me, and I bet it suits almost all people because I am just an ordinary guy, not brilliant at school ^-^.

How we can do it?

Know exactly Why-How-When!

Before learning anything, you must answer three questions:

  • Why I learning this?
  • How I can use this?
  • When I can use this?

Answer these questions to help you identify the purpose of the future duration you will spend to learn this new thing. So make sure you have a target to learn it, use it in the future, and use it regularly.

Know your learning Style

Check your VAK learning test! Once you know your affinities, use the right techniques and tools to maximize your understanding in record time.

There are eight forms of intelligence: spatial, bodily, musical, linguistic, logical, interpersonal, introspective, and naturalistic.

Turn dry information into Vivid Imagery

The brain is not good at holding raw information (equation, concepts, theorems), but extremely good at learning through vivid imagery and emotions.

Work from a Not-To-Do List

Treat your task equally. Many tasks that seem urgent, such as replying to emails and attending meetings, are not crucial to your learning goals. Such work only gives you a fake sense of productivity but doesn't help you make real progress.

“Practice Makes Perfect” Is a Myth

“Perfect practice makes it perfect.” — Jim Kwik.

Your most valuable asset is not time, it's attention. It doesn’t matter whether you have a thousand years if you waste them doing lazy work. So when doing your learning task, keep your mind focus and intensity, although give a small time on it. As a result, you’ll reap extraordinary results in the long run.
